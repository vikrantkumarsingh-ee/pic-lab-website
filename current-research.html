Current research

Precision instruments built from first principles

PIC Lab co-designs physical mechanisms, actuators, sensors, electronics, estimation methods, and feedback controllers for instruments that must move, image, measure, and interact at micro- and nanometre scales.

Shared research philosophy

One platform, several scientific translations

Contactless support

Reduce friction, wear, backlash, and guide-related coupling by separating support from conventional mechanical contact.

Programmable actuation and sensing

Co-design planar actuation, integrated force or position sensing, electronics, and real-time measurement.

Feedback and automation

Use control, machine vision, estimation, and experiment sequencing to transform prototypes into reproducible instruments.

Education and open hardware

Modular precision instrumentation and control platforms

PIC Lab plans to translate the same hardware philosophy into open experimental systems for control, sensing, embedded systems, and precision instrumentation.

The planned platforms will expose the actuator, sensor, signal-conditioning, data-acquisition, communication, and controller layers. Students will be able to characterize individual subsystems and then integrate a complete closed-loop instrument.
